Small house

Project Details

  • Detail design and construction supervision: Elena Vazquez and Julio Diarte
    • About:

    Year: 2015

    My husband and I married in 2015, and we decided to design and build a small house in the corner of her familyâ€™s property that could be rented on Airbnb once we travel to the US for grad school. We did not have much money and time, so we decided, firstly, to reuse and recycle every single building material we had left from buildings that our office constructed previously. Secondly, we used a fast-construction system based on steel framing enclosed with panels that could be easy to assembly.

    The design of the house is very simple; the first move was to elevate the house and leave the first floor as an open space where people can walk-through to go from one side of the property to the other. The second move was to place the living area on the second-floor (living, kitchen, and a small bathroom), and the third move was to place the bed on a mezzanine. We dedicated a few weeks for designing and planning and then eight weeks to complete the construction. We achieved to reduce the budget by 30% thanks to the reuse of recovered materials. The small house is still standing, and the Airbnb guest love it!
